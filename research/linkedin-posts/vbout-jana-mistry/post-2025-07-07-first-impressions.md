## Full Post Text:

In email, first impressions matter.

Jaina Mistry says that if you are sending more than 20 emails a day from one mailbox, you are slowly hurting your deliverability. 

That is because email providers watch sender behavior over time.

They care about whether people engage, whether messages look consistent, and whether you are acting like a sender people want to hear from. 

The bigger point is that deliverability is not just a technical problem.

It is a trust problem.

If your sending habits are sloppy, inbox providers notice. If your content is relevant and your cadence is steady, they notice that too. 

That is why good email marketing is built on discipline, not just volume.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Sending too many emails from one mailbox can hurt deliverability.
- Email providers watch sender behavior and engagement.
- Deliverability is a trust signal, not just a technical setup.
- Consistent, relevant sending is safer than high-volume chaos.

## How I'll use this:
I will be careful about sending patterns and treat inbox trust as something that has to be earned over time.
