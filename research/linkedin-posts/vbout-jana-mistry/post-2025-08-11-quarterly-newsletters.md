## Full Post Text:

Here to tell you that your quarterly newsletter is just not cutting it.

If your goal is to keep your brand top-of-mind, sending one newsletter every three months is not enough.

Jaina Mistry says the best cadence is whatever you can sustain while still creating great, valuable, interesting content. In practice, that usually means weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly. 

She also points out that newsletters are relationship builders.

If you only show up every quarter, you are not really building a relationship. You are reintroducing yourself every time. 

And there is a deliverability issue too.

Inbox service providers need consistent sending to learn that you are a trustworthy sender. Quarterly sends make that much harder. 

The larger point is simple: newsletters work best when they are consistent, useful, and worth showing up for.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Quarterly newsletters are too infrequent for brand awareness.
- Newsletters should keep your brand top-of-mind.
- Consistency helps both audience trust and deliverability.
- A newsletter should build a relationship, not check a box.

## How I'll use this:
I will choose a cadence I can sustain and make sure every newsletter has a clear purpose for the audience.
