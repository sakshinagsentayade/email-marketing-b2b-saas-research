## Full Post Text:

Great email deliverability isn’t just about getting into the inbox.

It’s about proving you deserve to be there.

Jana Mistry says the real driver of deliverability is engagement. Inbox providers watch how people interact with your emails, including opens, clicks, replies, and even whether they move your message out of spam. 

She also points out that relevance can beat personalization.

You do not need to force someone’s name into every subject line. You need to send content they actually care about.

And while technical setup matters, it is only the entry fee.

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are important, but they cannot save weak content or erratic sending patterns. 

The bigger lesson is simple: deliverability improves when your emails are consistent, useful, and worth opening.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Engagement is the strongest deliverability signal.
- Relevance matters more than shallow personalization.
- SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are necessary but not enough.
- Consistency and useful content protect inbox placement.

## How I'll use this:
I will focus on relevance and engagement first, then make sure the technical setup supports the strategy.
