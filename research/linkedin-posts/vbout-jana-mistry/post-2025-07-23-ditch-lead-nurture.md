## Full Post Text:

B2B marketers should ditch lead nurture for a newsletter.
Not a newsletter that exists only for leads.
A newsletter for the human beings who have shown some interest in your brand.
Jaina Mistry says a good newsletter is not just a place to dump product updates, company news, or recycled content. 
It needs:- A clear editorial point of view.- A consistent cadence.- Audience-first content.- A strong hook or intro.- Lightweight design.- Personality.- A feedback loop. 
Her larger point is that newsletters should build relationship and trust, not just automate touches.
That means writing for the reader first and using the format to create something people actually want to see in their inbox.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Lead nurture is not the same as a real newsletter.
- Newsletters should be built for people, not just leads.
- Editorial point of view matters.
- Strong newsletters need personality, cadence, and feedback.

## How I'll use this:
I will treat the newsletter as a relationship-building asset with a clear point of view, not just an automated nurture sequence.
