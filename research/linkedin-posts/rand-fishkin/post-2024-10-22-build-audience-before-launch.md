## Full Post Text:

Rand Fishkin’s advice is simple: build an audience before you build and launch your product.

Launching to an empty room makes everything harder.

Without an audience, even a good product can struggle to get attention, feedback, or early traction. 

His point is that a pre-launch audience gives you a base of people who already know you, trust you, and are more likely to care when you finally release something.

That makes launch marketing easier, feedback faster, and early growth more realistic. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Build audience first, then launch.
- Empty-room launches are risky.
- Pre-launch audiences improve trust and traction.
- Audience-building is part of product strategy.

## How I'll use this:
I will focus on growing an audience early so the product has people ready to care when it launches.
