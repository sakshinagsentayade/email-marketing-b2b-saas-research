## Full Post Text:

We are living in a Zero Click World.

Rand Fishkin says the things people used to do on your site have moved to the big tech platforms. 

That means the old marketing playbook does not work the same way anymore.

If you used to optimize for traffic, you now need to optimize for the platforms that influence your audience and the algorithms that decide what they see. 

His point is not that the internet is dying.

It is that discovery, attribution, and measurement have changed, and marketers need to change with them. 

The bigger lesson is simple: you cannot rely on the same web-first assumptions and expect the same results.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Zero-click behavior is now normal.
- Discovery happens on platforms, not just websites.
- Old traffic-first strategies are less effective.
- Marketers need to adapt to platform algorithms.

## How I'll use this:
I will think beyond website traffic and create content that performs where people already spend time.
