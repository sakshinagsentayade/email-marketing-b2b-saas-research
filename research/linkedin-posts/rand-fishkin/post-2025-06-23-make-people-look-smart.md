## Full Post Text:

Rand Fishkin says one of the key things brands should do is make influential people in their community look good when they talk about them. 

That means more than just amplifying someone’s work.

It means building a product, brand, or user experience that makes people look smart when they feature it. 

His bigger point is that word of mouth works best when sharing your brand makes the sharer look credible, insightful, or useful. 

When you build something people want to talk about and it makes them look smart for doing so, you unlock word of mouth on autopilot. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Word of mouth works better when sharers benefit socially.
- Brands should make influential people look smart.
- Product and experience matter as much as promotion.
- Great brand behavior creates repeat sharing.

## How I'll use this:
I will create content and products that make it easy for others to talk about me in a smart, useful way.
