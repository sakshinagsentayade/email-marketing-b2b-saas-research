# Post: Why Broad Content Often Fails

**Author:** Rand Fishkin  
**Date:** 2021-02-23  
**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/randfishkin_contentresearch-audienceinsights-activity-6770163170234519552-UZX5  
**Engagement:** LinkedIn post

---

## Full Post Text:

Rand Fishkin explains that content that tries to appeal to everyone often ends up landing with no one.
His point is that broad content can look smart, useful, or polished, but still fail to connect if it does not match the people who are most likely to care and share it. 
He calls this the paradox of content marketing to experts versus beginners.
To make content work, you need to understand your market’s amplifiers, why they amplify, and how to reach them. 
Without that research, a lot of effort gets wasted on social posts, blogs, and large studies that never really connect with the right audience. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Broad content often fails to connect.
- You need to know who will amplify your message.
- Audience research matters before publishing.
- Content works best when it is designed for a specific market.

## How I'll use this:
I will focus on the people most likely to care, share, and act instead of trying to make content appeal to everyone.
