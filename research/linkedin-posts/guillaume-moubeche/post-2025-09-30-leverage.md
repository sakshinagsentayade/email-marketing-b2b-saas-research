## Full Post Text:

You don’t need to “get rich.” You need leverage.

Here are 11 shifts that changed everything for me:

1. Pick one thing and go all in for a year.
When I launched lemlist, I said no to 5 other ideas I was excited about. Focusing on one is what gave us compounding traction.

2. Create before you consume.
Before posting anything online, I’d focus on growing the business first.

3. Know exactly what game you’re playing.
Some founders want freedom. Others want a $100M exit. I wanted leverage with full control.

4. Build things that run without you.
It took me 7 years to make lemlist run 100% without me.

5. Track inputs, not just outcomes.
In the early days, I tracked cold emails sent, open rates, and replies received.

6. Make your reputation louder than your resume.
I never pitched investors, but they DM’d me because I shared everything transparently on LinkedIn.

7. Treat energy like capital.
I stopped taking back-to-back calls and only work with people who energize me.

8. Design your life to reduce friction.
Same breakfast. Same calendar blocks. No endless decisions.

9. Audit your environment ruthlessly.
I unfollowed 90% of people online and stopped going to meetings where nothing moves.

10. Learn to love iteration.
Our first lemlist onboarding was terrible, so we kept improving it.

11. Build something that gives you options.
Money didn’t change me, but time and freedom did.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Focus on one thing instead of many.
- Track useful numbers, not vanity metrics.
- Build a business that gives freedom.
- Leverage matters more than just getting rich.

## How I'll use this:
I will focus on one goal, track the right numbers, and build skills that give me more options later.
