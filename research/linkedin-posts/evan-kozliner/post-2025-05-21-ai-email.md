## Full Post Text:

Most AI-generated emails read like a robot’s fever dream.

Evan Kozliner says the problem is not AI itself, but the way people use it.

His main points are:
- Generic subject lines do not work.
- Weak personalization feels fake.
- Value props need to sound human, not corporate.
- Smaller, niche newsletters and emails work better than mass outreach.

He says good AI-assisted writing should still sound like a real person wrote it.

The key is to use AI for support, not to replace judgment.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- AI emails should still sound human.
- Generic personalization is easy to spot.
- Niche content is stronger than broad content.
- Judgment matters more than automation.

## How I'll use this:
I will use AI to help draft emails, but I will edit them heavily so they sound natural and specific.
