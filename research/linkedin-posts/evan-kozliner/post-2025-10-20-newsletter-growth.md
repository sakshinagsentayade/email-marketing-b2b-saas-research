## Full Post Text:
We launched Aimply Briefs, a personalized AI-powered newsletter that could be about anything you care about.

You tell us a bit about yourself, and we build a newsletter just for you, delivered five days a week.

Our growth strategy was simple but powerful:
1. We found LinkedIn and Twitter threads where influencers were offering lead magnets.
2. These posts had hundreds or thousands of people commenting, showing interest in the topic.
3. We scraped the public lists of interested people.
4. We sent personalized cold emails saying we had a newsletter just for them, about the exact topic they wanted more of.

Because the newsletter could be customized for any topic, every thread became a potential list of subscribers.

Some days, we added 1,000 subscribers.

When we shut the product down, we had about 50,000 users and 25,000 daily active users.

The big idea is that intent-based marketing works well when people are already showing interest.

---
## Key Takeaways:
- Look for public signs of interest before outreach.
- Personalization makes outreach more relevant.
- A newsletter can grow fast if it matches existing demand.
- Intent-based marketing is powerful for B2B growth.

## How I'll use this:
I will remember to look for people already showing interest in a topic, then reach out with something valuable and specific.
