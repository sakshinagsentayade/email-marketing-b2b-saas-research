## Full Post Text:

Back in 2017, Drift’s CEO David Cancel said:

“The way we’ve been taught to work is perfectly suited for a world that no longer exists.”

That idea still matters.

David Cancel’s broader point is that B2B has been broken for a long time.

On most websites, the only way to get in touch with a company is through a form, and the only way companies collect details is through that same form. 

But forms are slow, awkward, and disconnected from what buyers actually want.

He helped popularize conversational marketing because real conversations are faster, more human, and more useful than forcing people through static capture flows. 

The bigger lesson is simple: if the buying experience has changed, the way we work and market should change too.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Old B2B workflows were built for a different era.
- Forms create friction for buyers.
- Conversational experiences feel more natural.
- Good marketing follows how people actually want to interact.

## How I'll use this:
I will look for ways to replace rigid workflows with more natural, conversational experiences for buyers.
