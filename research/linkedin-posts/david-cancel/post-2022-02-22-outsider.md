# Post: The Perks of Being an Outsider

**Author:** David Cancel  
**Date:** 2022-02-22  
**Link:** https://sequoiacap.com/article/david-cancel-spotlight/  
**Engagement:** Founder spotlight

---

## Full Post Text:

David Cancel talks about how being an outsider shaped the way he thinks and builds.

Growing up, he learned that success in technology did not depend on having the “right” background. What mattered was problem solving, adaptability, and the ability to learn quickly. 

He says that feeling like an outsider gave him humility, empathy, and determination.

Instead of trying to fit in, he learned to question assumptions and look for better ways to do things. 

That mindset became a strength as he built companies.

It helped him stay curious, challenge the status quo, and keep moving when the path was not obvious. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Outsiders often see problems differently.
- Adaptability matters more than credentials.
- Humility and curiosity can become strengths.
- Questioning norms can lead to better products.

## How I'll use this:
I will treat outsider perspective as an advantage and use it to ask better questions and find better solutions.
