## Full Post Text:

The founders who last are the ones who keep moving while the ground shifts underneath them.

David Cancel’s point is that the founder badge matters less than the ability to adapt, learn, and keep building when the old playbook stops working. 

He has spent much of his career challenging assumptions in SaaS, product, and marketing.

That includes questioning rigid workflows, outdated buying experiences, and systems built for a different era. 

The lesson is not to chase certainty.

It is to stay curious, stay flexible, and keep looking for the better way when the world changes. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Longevity comes from adaptability.
- Founders need to question default systems.
- Curiosity is more valuable than certainty.
- The best operators keep moving as conditions change.

I will stay flexible, question outdated assumptions, and keep looking for better ways to build and grow.
