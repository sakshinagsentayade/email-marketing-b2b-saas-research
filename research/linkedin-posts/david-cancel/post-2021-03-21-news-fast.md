## Full Post Text:

David Cancel explains why he went on a news fast and why it helped him think more clearly.

His main point is that constant news consumption can overload your attention with negativity, noise, and other people’s opinions. 

Instead of staying plugged into everything, he argues for being more selective about what you consume and when you consume it. 

The idea is not to be uninformed.

It is to be intentional about your mental input so you can stay focused on what matters most. 

That fits his broader founder perspective: build with clarity, avoid distraction, and stay disciplined about where your attention goes. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Constant news can create mental noise.
- Being selective with information improves focus.
- The goal is intentionality, not ignorance.
- Discipline around attention matters for founders.

## How I'll use this:
I will be more intentional about what I consume so I can protect focus and make better decisions.
