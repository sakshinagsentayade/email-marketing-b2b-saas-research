## Full Post Text:

I bootstrapped my startup to $22m ARR in 4 years.

People tell me they would LOVE to bootstrap rather than take VC money, but they don't know how to do it.

If you're at $0 MRR, here are 4 ways you can fund your startup:

1. Exclusively friends and family.
2. Start a consultancy or agency to fund your SaaS.
3. Keep your job.
4. Use customer revenue as soon as possible.

The main idea is to grow a solid, profitable software company without giving up control too early.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Bootstrapping means growing without outside investors.
- Customer revenue can fund growth.
- You can start small and still build something big.
- Keeping control can be valuable.

## How I'll use this:
I will remember that a business can grow from customer money instead of outside funding, which gives more control.
