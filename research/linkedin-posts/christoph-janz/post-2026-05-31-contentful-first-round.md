## Full Post Text:

Christoph Janz shared that Salesforce’s acquisition of Contentful brought back memories of one of Point Nine’s earliest bets.

He wrote that Point Nine co-led Contentful’s first financing round in 2012, after a cold email from founder Sascha Konietzke. At that point, Contentful was still called StorageRoom, had 28 paying customers, and only a few hundred euros of MRR. 

He also noted that the company originally consisted only of Sascha, with Paolo Negri joining soon after. 

His message is a reminder that great companies often start very small, and that early belief can matter as much as early traction. 

The bigger lesson is that long-term conviction, strong founder relationships, and willingness to back tiny beginnings can lead to huge outcomes.

## Key Takeaways:
- Great companies often begin with very little traction.
- Early investor conviction can matter a lot.
- Founder cold outreach can open major doors.
- Patience and long-term thinking are important in SaaS investing.
