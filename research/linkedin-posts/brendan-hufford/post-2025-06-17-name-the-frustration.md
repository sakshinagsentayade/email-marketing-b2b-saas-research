## Full Post Text:
I posted a rant.
No CTA. No creative. Just a sentence about how digital marketing makes me feel lately.
It blew up.
Brendan Hufford says the bigger lesson is that your customers do not need you to name your solution. They need you to name their frustration.
That is what he calls Content IP: giving people language for the problem they already feel.
We remember problems more than products because problems are easier to feel, share, and talk about.
He points out that the strongest content often starts with emotion and recognition, not polish.
That is why clarity matters so much:- Name the frustration.- Use language people already feel.- Make the problem easy to recognize.- Let the audience say, “That’s exactly it.”

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Problems stick better than product features.
- Naming frustration creates resonance.
- Emotion makes content more shareable.
- Clear problem language builds Content IP.

## How I'll use this:I will write more content that names the frustration my audience already feels, instead of leading with my own solution.
