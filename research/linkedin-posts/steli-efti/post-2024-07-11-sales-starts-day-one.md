# Post: Sales Starts on Day One

**Author:** Steli Efti  
**Date:** 2024-07-11  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rfh8BRrqmhU  
**Engagement:** Interview / LinkedIn-style content

## Full Post Text:

Steli Efti’s core message is simple: founders do sales from day one.

You may not call it sales, but the moment you explain your idea, convince someone to join, win investors, or talk to customers, you are selling. 

His point is that sales is really result-driven communication.

If you are trying to help someone make a decision, create clarity, or move them toward action, that is sales in practice. 

He also argues that founders should accept this early instead of resisting it.

The faster you accept that sales is part of building a company, the faster you can get better at it. 
---

## Key Takeaways:
- Founders are selling from day one.
- Sales is result-driven communication.
- Clarity and persuasion are part of the founder role.
- Accepting sales early makes growth easier.

## How I'll use this:
I will treat sales as a core part of building, not as something separate from product or strategy.
