## Full Post Text:

90% of sales reps are killing their deals in the inbox.

Steli Efti says you should never negotiate pricing via email because email negotiations are where deals go to die. 

Those threads become slow, messy, and drawn out when the same conversation could be handled in a live call. 

His point is that sales requires discomfort.

Instead of hiding behind email, you should pick up the phone, talk like a human, handle objections in real time, and get to the truth faster. 

The bigger lesson is simple: stop avoiding the hard conversation.

Start selling. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Pricing should not be negotiated in email.
- Live conversations close deals faster.
- Avoiding discomfort hurts sales.
- Real-time objection handling is more effective.

## How I'll use this:
I will move pricing and negotiation discussions out of email and into live conversations whenever possible.
