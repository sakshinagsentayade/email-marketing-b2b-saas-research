## Full Post Text:

The actual subject line of the email my co-founder Anthony sent to a prospect after we completely botched their trial experience was: “We Fucked Up.” 

Here’s what happened.

A great company was trialing their product and had lots of technical questions for the team. But instead of helping them succeed, the team sent them into handoff hell. One rep could not answer the questions and passed them to someone else. That person turned out to be on vacation. 

Anthony did not hide from it or blame other people.

He took responsibility and wrote a candid email with the subject line “We Fucked Up.” It got the prospect’s attention. 

The lesson is that trust is built when you own mistakes directly and clearly.

Sometimes honesty is the strongest sales move you can make. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Owning mistakes builds trust.
- Direct honesty can outperform polished excuses.
- Sales is not just persuasion; it is accountability.
- A candid message can cut through noise.

## How I'll use this:
I will be direct when something goes wrong and take responsibility quickly instead of hiding behind vague language.
