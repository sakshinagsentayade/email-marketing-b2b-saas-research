## Full Post Text:

What’s the latest “black box” sales hack that gets cold prospects to ACTUALLY listen?

Steli Efti says it is not a complicated trick.

It is making the message easy to engage with. 

His example is a voice memo.

Text feels easy to ignore, and long paragraphs feel like work. But a short voice memo creates curiosity and lowers friction because it is different and quick to consume. 

The bigger lesson is that prospects respond when outreach feels human, simple, and low effort.

If you make them work too hard, they will tune out. 

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Simplicity beats complicated outreach tricks.
- Low-friction messages get more attention.
- Human formats can feel more engaging.
- Prospects ignore messages that feel like work.

## How I'll use this:
I will make outreach easy to consume and avoid formats that force the prospect to do too much work.
