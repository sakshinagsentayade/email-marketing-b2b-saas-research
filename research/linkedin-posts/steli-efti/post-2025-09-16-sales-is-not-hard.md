## Full Post Text:
Steli Efti says sales is not hard.
What is hard is people: their doubts, hesitations, fear of rejection, and ego. 
His point is that the actual work of sales is simple.
The challenge comes from discomfort, because meaningful sales actions usually push you outside your comfort zone. 
That includes following up again, asking the blunt question, and making the call you would rather avoid. 
His advice is to treat pain and discomfort as a signal that you are probably doing the right thing.

---

## Key Takeaways:
- Sales feels hard mostly because of discomfort.
- The work itself is usually simple.
- Fear and hesitation slow sellers down.
- Discomfort can be a useful signal.

## How I'll use this:
I will stop treating discomfort as a warning sign and start treating it as part of the sales process.
